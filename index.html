Media and Information Literacy (MIL) Lesson 5

These lessons cover key sourcing methods in MIL, focusing on credible and culturally relevant information sources.

Sourcing Indigenous Media

Indigenous is a native, local, originating or produced naturally in a particular region and locality. Its indigenous knowledge is unique to a special culture or society, most often it is not written down. The transmission of information is through local channels or forms. It is a means by which the culture is preserved, handed down and adapted.

  • Highly credible due to proximity to the source and non-profit circulation.
  • Channels for education, change, and development in rural areas unreachable by mainstream media.
  • In the Philippines, supports cultural pride and knowledge transmission through community-based approaches.

Sourcing in Library and Prints

Library is a place in which literary, musical, artistic, or reference materials (such as books, manuscripts, recordings, or films) are kept for use but not for sale.

  • Academic, public, school, and special libraries organize information for critical evaluation.
  • Librarians teach evaluation criteria like authority, accuracy, and relevance via worksheets.
  • Builds skills in understanding media techniques and impact.

Sourcing on the Internet and Online Resources

A global computer network providing a variety of information and communication facilities, consisting of interconnected networks using standardized communication protocols.

  • Check source reliability, author credentials, and publisher information; avoid anonymous sites like Wikipedia for primary research.
  • Search for keywords effectively and verify timeliness and objectivity.
  • Applies to databases, websites, and digital libraries for informed media choices.

Sourcing from Other Platforms

Other platforms include community media, magazines , newspapers, social media, and encyclopedias are integrated for information access.

  • Evaluate using similar criteria: accuracy, value, authority, and timeliness.
  • Platforms like digital libraries, and initiatives from organizations like UNESCO enhance competencies.
  • Supports intercultural dialogue and critical use across all media forms.
