In this lesson, you will learn how to find reliable information from different sources and how to decide which sources you can trust.

üìå Sourcing Indigenous Media

Indigenous media comes from local or native communities. This information is often shared through stories, traditions, music, or community activities. It helps preserve culture and history.

Why is this important?
  • Information comes directly from the community, making it reliable.
  • It helps people in rural areas learn and stay informed.
  • In the Philippines, it strengthens cultural identity and pride.

üìö Sourcing in Libraries and Print Materials

Libraries provide books, newspapers, magazines, and other printed materials that are carefully selected and organized for learning and research.

Remember:
  • Library materials are reviewed before being made available.
  • Librarians can help you check if information is accurate and trustworthy.
  • Using library sources improves your research skills.

üåê Sourcing on the Internet and Online Resources

The internet gives access to a large amount of information, but not everything online is true. You must learn how to check if a source is reliable.

Tips for using online sources:
  • Check who wrote the information and when it was published.
  • Use trusted websites and databases.
  • Compare information from different sources.

üì± Sourcing from Other Platforms

Information can also come from newspapers, magazines, social media, community media, and encyclopedias. Each platform should be evaluated carefully.

Always ask yourself:
  • Is the information accurate?
  • Who created it and why?
  • Is it updated and relevant?
