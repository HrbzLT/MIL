Media and Information Literacy (MIL) Lessons

These lessons cover key sourcing methods in MIL, focusing on credible and culturally relevant information sources.

Sourcing Indigenous Media

Indigenous media are owned, controlled, and managed by indigenous peoples to produce culturally appropriate information using local materials and languages, reflecting community needs without external vested interests.[web:11][web:5]

  • Highly credible due to proximity to the source and non-profit circulation.[web:5]
  • Channels for education, change, and development in rural areas unreachable by mainstream media.[web:5]
  • In the Philippines, supports cultural pride and knowledge transmission through community-based MIL approaches.[web:11]

Sourcing in Library and Prints

Libraries provide print and non-print media resources selected using media literacy skills to disseminate reliable information.[web:2]

  • Academic, public, school, and special libraries organize information for critical evaluation.[web:9]
  • Librarians teach evaluation criteria like authority, accuracy, and relevance via worksheets.[web:6]
  • Builds skills in understanding media techniques and impact.[web:2]

Sourcing on the Internet and Online Resources

Evaluate online sources using criteria like CRAAP (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose) to ensure credibility.[web:12][web:15]

  • Check source reliability, author credentials, and publisher information; avoid anonymous sites like Wikipedia for primary research.[web:3]
  • Search for keywords effectively and verify timeliness and objectivity.[web:7]
  • Applies to databases, websites, and digital libraries for informed media choices.[web:4]

Sourcing from Other Platforms

Other platforms include community media, archives, museums, social media, and NGOs, integrated into MIL for diverse information access.[web:4][web:16]

  • Evaluate using similar criteria: accuracy, value, authority, and timeliness.[web:9]
  • Platforms like MOOCs, digital libraries, and initiatives from organizations like UNESCO enhance MIL competencies.[web:13]
  • Supports intercultural dialogue and critical use across all media forms.[web:1]
