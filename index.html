üìå Sourcing Indigenous Media

Indigenous media comes from local or native communities. This information is often shared through stories, traditions, music, and community practices.

Why this matters:
  • Information comes directly from the community.
  • It helps preserve culture and traditions.
  • It supports cultural pride, especially in the Philippines.

üìö Sourcing in Libraries and Print Materials

Libraries provide books, newspapers, and magazines that are reviewed and organized for research and learning.

Remember:
  • Library sources are usually reliable.
  • Librarians help check accuracy and credibility.
  • Using library sources improves research skills.

üåê Sourcing on the Internet and Online Resources

The internet contains useful information, but not everything online is true. Students must evaluate online sources carefully.

Online safety tips:
  • Check the author and publication date.
  • Use trusted websites and databases.
  • Compare information from different sources.

üì± Sourcing from Other Platforms

Information also comes from newspapers, magazines, social media, and community media.

Ask yourself:
  • Is the information accurate?
  • Who created it and why?
  • Is it updated and relevant?
